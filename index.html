Nitrate-Free Sausages

Why should I eat Nitrate-Free Sausages?

In the production of products like bacon, ham and hot dogs, sodium nitrite is added as a preservative, which helps prevent the growth of the deadly botulism bacteria and provides the characteristic pink color and piquant "cured" flavor to these meat products.

Nitrosamines are created when sodium nitrite is heated to a particularly high temperature and they have been linked in studies to cancer, and so they are considered carcinogenic. Because of these studies, the USDA has imposed limits on the amount of sodium nitrite in cured meats and requires the addition of ascorbic acid (an antioxidant) in order to restrict the nitrosamines that we consume.
